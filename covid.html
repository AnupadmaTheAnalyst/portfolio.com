üìä Case Study: From Data to Public Health Intelligence

Business / Research Challenge

Governments, hospitals, and policy teams needed insights to understand the severity of the pandemic. Key analytical questions included:

  • Which countries had the highest infection and death rates?
  • How fast were populations getting vaccinated?
  • Which regions showed the highest risk?
  • What trends emerged in cumulative vaccination progress?

Analytical Approach

  • Cleaned & standardized missing data (continent NULL handling)
  • Used aggregations to calculate infection and death percentages
  • Performed joins between deaths & vaccination tables
  • Created CTEs and Window Functions for rolling vaccination counts
  • Built temporary tables for deeper exploration
  • Created a reusable view for population vaccination percentage

Key Insights

  • Some nations experienced infection rates above 30% of their total population.
  • Death rates varied significantly by continent, highlighting resource inequality.
  • Rolling vaccination analysis revealed large differences in vaccine rollout speed.
  • Global mortality percentage provided perspective on pandemic severity.

Business / Policy Impact

This SQL exploration demonstrates analytical competency in handling large datasets, extracting meaningful health insights, and building structures (CTEs, Views, Temp Tables) that support scalable reporting and future dashboarding.
