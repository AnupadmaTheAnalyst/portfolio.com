üìä Case Study: Turning Sales Data into Business Intelligence

Business Challenge

Sales leadership and operations teams need clear answers to questions such as:

  • Which product lines and deal sizes drive the most revenue?
  • Who are the highest-value customers?
  • How does revenue trend across years and seasons?
  • Which regions and products carry higher cancellation risk?
  • Are customer spending patterns improving over time?

Analytical Approach

  • Analyzed revenue trends by year, month, product line, and deal size
  • Segmented customers based on order frequency and revenue contribution
  • Used subqueries and window functions for ranking and trend analysis
  • Identified high-performing products and regions
  • Evaluated cancellation patterns to highlight operational risks
  • Applied CTEs and window functions for advanced business insights

Key Business Insights

  • Large deal sizes contribute a disproportionately high share of total revenue.
  • A small segment of customers accounts for a significant portion of sales.
  • Revenue shows clear seasonal patterns across months and years.
  • Certain regions and product lines exhibit higher cancellation rates.
  • Some customers demonstrate increasing average order value over time.

Business Impact

This analysis enables data-driven decisions for sales strategy, customer prioritization, inventory planning, and operational improvement. The structured SQL queries can be reused for ongoing performance monitoring and reporting.

These insights help leadership optimize pricing strategies, focus on high-value customers, and reduce revenue leakage caused by cancellations.
