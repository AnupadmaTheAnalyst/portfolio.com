üìä Case Study: Optimizing Inventory & Supply Chain Operations

Business Challenge

Organizations often struggle with excess inventory, underutilized warehouse capacity, delayed deliveries, and rising logistics costs. Decision-makers lacked a centralized dashboard to track inventory health, fulfillment performance, and supply chain efficiency in real time.

Key Business Questions

  • Is warehouse capacity being efficiently utilized?
  • How quickly is inventory moving through the supply chain?
  • Which product categories experience longer lead times?
  • What drives backorders and pending orders?
  • Where can transportation and logistics costs be optimized?

Analytical Approach

  • Modeled inventory and supply chain data using a structured data model
  • Cleaned and transformed raw data using Power Query
  • Created DAX measures for inventory and operational KPIs
  • Designed interactive dashboards for performance monitoring
  • Enabled regional and category-level drill-down analysis

Key Business Insights

  • Warehouse utilization remains below optimal levels, indicating unused capacity.
  • Inventory turnover highlights opportunities to reduce holding costs.
  • Specific product categories show higher average lead times, increasing fulfillment risk.
  • Backorders are largely driven by demand-supply mismatches.
  • Transportation costs vary significantly across regions.

Business Impact

These insights support better inventory planning, warehouse capacity optimization, and supply chain cost control. Businesses can proactively reduce backorders, improve fulfillment performance, and align inventory levels with demand.

The dashboard enables data-driven decisions for operations, logistics planning, and supply chain strategy.
